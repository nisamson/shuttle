# Tales From The Datacube, Part 2: Goalies Should Scare You

## Introduction
This will be shorter than my [last entry](https://simulationhockey.com/showthread.php?tid=149517&pid=3859505#pid3859505), as there are fewer stats and goalies typically aren't divided into as many categories
as skaters. If you know any hockey goaltenders in real life, you'll probably know they tend to be underappreciated: they get blamed when the team loses
and rarely share as strongly in the glory of the victories unless it's a shutout or a shootout.

The American Association for the Prevention of Cruelty to Goalies recommends buying all of your beer league team's goalies' drinks. Seriously, treat them nicely!
I've heard too many stories of goalies being treated poorly by their teammates.

## Goalie Categories

Last time, I focused solely on skaters when creating categories. That was in part to keep that post from becoming unreadably long
and in part to give myself time to investigate goalies more thoroughly. Their stats are very different, so I came into this analysis
with fewer preconceived notions. I let the computer divide goaltenders into two categories, and here are the results:

![goalie_categories.png](https://imgur.com/GGdLVkj.png)

After the computer spat this out, my spouse and I were impressed at how the categories separated out!
It identified two categories: puckhandling goaltenders and the windmillers,
Brodeurs and Haseks, two goalies brought into the borderline religious debates on who is the best goaltender of all time.
Patrick Roy is obviously in that conversation as well, but I had to pick representatives for these categories and went with Hasek.

Sue me.

### Category A: Dominik Hasek - Never Give Up!

Domink Hasek was known for his wild desperation saves, sticking with a play no matter
how many times he had to dive and windmill across the crease. Category A clearly reflects his style of play
with emphasis on reflexes and using every part of the body he can to make it to the puck.

Goalies like Hasek are both [terrifying and electrifying to watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YBA_k1NSgnI).
I got sidetracked for, like, 20 minutes just watching his highlights, go check them out.

Hasek is represented by his medoid, [Enzo Reaper](https://portal.simulationhockey.com/player/2643), who just got drafted
in S89 to the Montreal Patriotes!

### Category B: Martin Brodeur - Roam Wild and Free!

Why make a save when you can just get the puck out of the zone?

Martin left his crease *a lot*, **way** more than other goaltenders of the time. He was so effective at doing so that the NHL
told him and his ilk to [put a stop to that](https://allblackhockeysticks.com/the-trapezoid-rule-why-goalies-cant-roam-like-they-used-to/) and
instituted the trapezoid rule prohibiting goalies from playing the puck in the corners.

Puckhandling goalies, as the article linked mentions, prevent effective zone entries by getting the puck back up ice before
the other team can effectively establish their presence in the zone.

It takes some guts and skill to leave the net to make a play, but it contributed to an extremely effective
Devils defense in the era before the 2004-05 lockout which resulted in the new rule limiting it.


Brodeur is represented by his medoid... [Bandit Heeler](https://portal.simulationhockey.com/player/1058)? An Australian anthropomorphic blue heeler
isn't who I would expect to be a puckhandling goaltender, but he is known for his [unconventional fathering tactics](https://youtu.be/AMppfY8aF6M?si=EZ-qAdozM3UIJK0V&t=267),
so maybe this is in his wheelhouse.
